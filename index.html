About the programme

What a Skill Development Centre actually is

The policy context behind the courses running at GVHSS Chettiyankinar.

Samagra Shiksha Kerala (SSK)

Skill Development Centres operate as a major vocational intervention under Samagra Shiksha Kerala, the state programme run by the Department of General Education. SDCs are built to close the gap between what a traditional classroom teaches and what the modern job market actually asks for.

The STARS Project

Funding and execution run through the central STARS Project — Strengthening Teaching-Learning and Results for States. In practice, that means the equipment, trainers, and curriculum at this centre are backed by a national-level programme, not a one-school initiative.

Who it's for

The centre is built for students up to the higher secondary level, giving them advanced, future-ready job skills alongside their regular Plus One / Plus Two studies — no need to choose between board exams and employability.

This centre — GVHSS Chettiyankinar

SDC GVHSS Chettiyankinar runs two tracks on campus: Cloud Computing and Graphic Designing. Both are taught hands-on, with lab access and project-based assessment, so students leave with a portfolio — not just a certificate.

Courses on offer

Two tracks. Two very different toolkits.

Leads toward: graphic design, social media/content design, freelance design work.
